Creative boundaries

Do’s and Don’ts.

BOUNDARY FILE
02

Note: I reserve the right to decline any commission request that falls outside my skill range or makes me uncomfortable.

If you do not see something you are looking for in these lists, feel free to ask—I am always open to discussing ideas.

Please keep in mind that I am constantly improving my craft. While I strive to deliver my best work, some listed subjects may still be areas I am practicing.

✓

Accepted subjects

Things I can work with

  • Personal and commercial projects
  • SFW content: General, non-explicit artwork.
  • NSFW or suggestive: Minimal sexual or mature themes portrayed safely and respectfully. NSFW commissions require age verification before acceptance.
  • Humans: Including kemonomimi, hybrids, and humans with additional features.
  • Anthropomorphic characters: Furries and similar designs.
  • Creatures: Animals, ferals, monsters, and fantasy species.
  • Slight gore: Light blood, scars, or minor injuries.
  • Robots, mecha, and armor designs
×

Declined subjects

Things I will not accept

  • Long-term projects: Multiple comic pages, lengthy animations, and similarly extended commitments.
  • Unhealthy NSFW: Non-consensual acts, sexual violence, exploitation, coercion, or sexual content involving underage characters.
  • Heavy gore: Exposed internal organs or extreme mutilation and disfigurement.
  • NFT use: Non-fungible tokens or mass resale and distribution to multiple owners.
  • Political themes or hateful content: Racism, homophobia, transphobia, or related hateful material.
  • AI training: Using the artwork to train, generate, or feed into AI models or datasets.

Working together

Guide & Notes.

PROJECT FILE
03

  1. 01

    Communication

    Please provide clear references, descriptions, and details of what you want. This helps ensure accurate results and avoids misunderstandings.

  2. 02

    Revisions

    Minor revisions are allowed during the sketch and coloring stages. Major changes after finalization may incur an additional fee.

  3. 03

    Deadlines

    Turnaround time may vary depending on workload, complexity, or project type. Tell the artist about a specific deadline before booking so availability can be confirmed.

  4. 04

    Commercial vs. Personal Use

    Personal use includes profile pictures, wallpapers, personal displays, and similar use.

    Commercial use includes merchandise, promotional materials, or content that generates income. Please clarify the intended use when ordering.

  5. 05

    Respect & Boundaries

    Please remain respectful throughout communication. Rude, disrespectful, threatening, or excessively demanding behavior will not be tolerated.

Terms of Service.

TERMS FILE
04

By commissioning the artist, the client agrees to the following terms. A request is not automatically accepted; project-specific details may be confirmed privately before work begins.

01

Usage Rights

  • The commissioned artwork may be used for personal or agreed commercial purposes, provided clear artist credit is given where applicable.
  • Do not claim, trace, heavily edit, or modify the artwork without the artist’s permission.
  • Contact the artist for assistance or approval before making small edits or adjustments.
02

Payment

  • Payment may be made partially at 50% or in full before work begins, depending on the project’s complexity.
  • All payment must be completed before the final artwork is delivered.
  • The artist will provide accepted payment methods privately before starting the commission.
03

Refund Policy

  • Full refunds are available only before any work has started.
  • Partial refunds may be offered for projects in progress that have not yet been completed.
  • No refunds will be issued after the artwork is fully completed and delivered.
04

Cancellations & Violations

  • The artist may cancel a commission at any stage if the client violates the terms, behaves inappropriately, or makes unreasonable demands.
  • Breaking these terms may result in cancellation, refusal of future commissions, blocking, or blacklisting.
05

Copyright & Ownership

  • The artist retains copyright over the artwork, including the right to post, use, or display it in portfolios, examples, and promotion.
  • Clients may request that a commission remain private, but this must be discussed before work begins.
Questions are welcome.

Ask for clarification before paying or approving the commission if any term is unclear.

Before you submit

Give me enough to understand the vision.

You do not need professional art terminology. Character references, sketches, playlists, color palettes, and plain descriptions are useful.

Payment verification

Selecting “I already paid” does not automatically prove payment. It marks the request as payment claimed. Haden will check PayPal or Ko-fi and manually change it to paid — verified.

  • Explain what you want drawn.
  • Include reference links when possible.
  • State personal or commercial use.
  • Mention a deadline before booking.
  • Do not include passwords or card information.
